# voice.md — example

> This is a filled-in example for a fictional fintech product called Clearline. Use it as a reference when completing your own voice.md.

---

## Personality

- **Direct** — but never blunt. Gets to the point without making people feel rushed.
- **Calm** — but never cold. Measured and steady, not distant.
- **Honest** — but never harsh. Says what's true, including when things go wrong.
- **Understated** — but never flat. Quiet confidence, not performed excitement.

---

## Tonal range

| Context | Register | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing / campaign | Confident, a little dry, no hype | Sounds like someone who knows their stuff, not someone selling |
| Product UI / labels | Clear, minimal, neutral | Function first — character only where it helps |
| Onboarding | Calm, welcoming, practical | Reduce friction, don't over-celebrate |
| Error messages | Direct, factual, never apologetic | Name the problem, give the fix, move on |
| Support / help content | Patient, clear, human | Step by step, no condescension |
| Empty states | Brief, useful, low-key | One line. Point somewhere. |

---

## Mechanics

**Person and address**
- Second person throughout — "your account", "you've paid", "check your balance"
- No first-person plural — avoid "we've made it easy" framing

**Sentence structure**
- Short by default. One idea. Then the next.
- No sentence over 20 words in UI copy.
- Rhythm matters — vary length, but never ramble.

**Punctuation and formatting**
- No exclamation marks. Anywhere.
- Oxford comma always.
- Em dash for parenthetical asides — brackets feel bureaucratic.
- Sentence case for headings and labels.

**Grammar**
- Active voice. Always.
- Contractions fine — "you're", "it's", "here's".
- Don't open with "We" — centers the product, not the person.
- Avoid progressive tense — "your transfer completed" not "your transfer is completing".

---

## Vocabulary

**Use**

| Word / phrase | Why |
|---|---|
| "paid" | Clearer than "transaction processed" |
| "something went wrong" | Honest without being alarming |
| "check" | Friendlier than "verify" or "confirm" |
| "here's what happened" | Opens an explanation without drama |
| "Clearline" | Always in full, always capitalised |

**Avoid**

| Word / phrase | Why |
|---|---|
| "seamless" | Empty |
| "powerful" | Vague |
| "we're sorry" | Apologetic without being useful |
| "please" | Polite filler — remove it |
| "leverage" | Corporate register |
| "easy" | Presumptuous — the user decides if it's easy |
| "exciting" | We don't get excited on the user's behalf |

**Brand-specific terms**

| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| Clearline | Full name always. Never "CL", never "the app". |
| Transfer | Not "payment" unless it's a bill payment specifically |
| Balance | Not "funds" or "money" |

---

## Reference

**This is the voice:**

> Your transfer to Sarah arrived. She'll see it within a few minutes.
>
> We couldn't process this one — your account balance was too low at the time. The money hasn't moved. Try again or adjust the amount.

**This is not the voice:**

> 🎉 Great news! Your payment was successfully processed and sent to Sarah! She should receive the funds shortly.
>
> We're so sorry, but unfortunately we were unable to complete your transaction at this time due to insufficient funds. Please ensure your balance is sufficient before attempting again. We apologise for any inconvenience caused.

*What's wrong with the second version: it performs emotion the user doesn't feel, apologises instead of informing, uses passive constructions, and buries the action in filler.*
